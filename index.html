Why this exists

The advisory gap nobody talks about

122M+ Registered investors in India

Commission-driven advice

Most financial advice in India is driven by product commissions — not your goals. Investors end up in high-cost mutual funds, ULIPs, and products that benefit the seller more than the buyer.

Information asymmetry

Quality research has traditionally been reserved for institutional and high-net-worth investors. Retail investors are left with unverified social media tips and sensational market commentary.

Too few advisers

India has over 122 million registered investors but fewer than 1,000 SEBI-registered Investment Advisers and about 1,750 Research Analysts. The gap is enormous.

Noise over substance

The market is saturated with low-quality stock tips, paid promotions, and unreliable "gurus." It is nearly impossible for a regular investor to find unbiased, research-backed guidance.
